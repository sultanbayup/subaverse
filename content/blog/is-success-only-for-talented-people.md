We hear the same idea over and over: success belongs to talented people. The "naturals". The ones who got it figured out early. I don't buy it, and I want to talk about why.

## What success even means

Everyone defines success differently.

For some it's money, a dream job, or a top-tier school. For others it's health, a family, or feeling at peace with the work they do. There is no single yardstick — it depends on what you actually value.

That alone makes "you need talent to succeed" a weak claim. Talent for what? Measured against whose definition?

## Talent is not enough

People love to say:

> If you want to succeed, you need talent.

Sure, talent helps. But it isn't the deciding factor. Plenty of talented people stall out. Plenty of "ordinary" people quietly compound effort over years and end up doing remarkable things.

Talent gives you a head start. Hard work, discipline, and a bit of luck decide where you actually finish.

## Showing up matters

Woody Allen put it bluntly:

> 80% of success is showing up.

I read "showing up" as taking action and putting in the work — repeatedly, especially when no one is watching. What looks like talent from the outside is usually consistent effort that nobody bothered to count.

## Just start

If there's one thing I've learned, it's this: you don't need to wait for the perfect moment, or for talent to "appear". Start with what you have.

- Do work that interests you.
- Don't be afraid to fail.
- Treat failure as part of the process, not the opposite of it.

Thomas Edison failed more than a thousand times before getting the light bulb to work. He's quoted as saying:

> I didn't fail 1,000 times. The light bulb was an invention with 1,000 steps.

That's the mindset.

## Final thoughts

So is success only for the talented? No.

Success comes from many directions — hard work, persistence, creativity, learning from mistakes. Talent is a head start, not a finish line.

What about you? Do you think success is reserved for the talented, or do you think anyone can build it on their own terms?
